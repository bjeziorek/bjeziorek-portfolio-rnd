Case Study — MAZE / SelfScape

Designing an adaptive cognitive architecture for users with fluctuating energy, motivation, and emotional load.

Context

Neurodivergent‑Friendly Planning System

SelfScape is a cognitive R&D project focused on creating a planning system that works for neurodivergent users who struggle with linear calendars, time‑based pressure, and inconsistent energy levels.

Problem

Avoidance, Overwhelm & Shutdown

Traditional planning tools trigger avoidance, overwhelm, and shutdown in ND users. The challenge was to design a system that adapts to fluctuating cognitive and emotional states.

How I Applied SPINE

SPINE in MAZE / SelfScape

Systems
Mapping Real ND Environments

I mapped the real environment of ND users: irregular days, unpredictable energy, emotional volatility, and the need for flexibility. This revealed that linear time‑based planning was fundamentally incompatible with their lived experience.

Prediction
Neurocognitive Models

I built neurocognitive models for ADHD, ASD, PDA, and chronic fatigue profiles. These models predicted that users would avoid rigid structures, abandon plans after disruptions, and experience guilt spirals triggered by traditional calendars.

Intervention
Designing MAZE Architecture

I designed MAZE — a cognitive architecture based on agent‑like tasks, emotional load propagation, and adaptive views. SelfScape became the first implementation of MAZE, offering fractal task decomposition, narrative‑based planning, and context‑aware visualisations.

Neurocognition
Modelling Attention & Emotional Load

I modelled attention dynamics, overwhelm thresholds, emotional load, and narrative thinking patterns. This allowed the system to adapt to the user’s internal state rather than forcing the user to adapt to the system.

During the neurocognitive modelling phase, I used CIR and FEDRI to map regulation load, avoidance loops, overwhelm thresholds, and narrative‑based decision patterns.

Environment
Validation in Real ND Daily Life

I validated prototypes in real ND daily life: inconsistent routines, sudden shutdowns, bursts of hyperfocus, and emotional turbulence. The system had to remain stable even when input data was chaotic.

Impact

Behavioural & Cognitive Outcomes

  • A planning system that reduces avoidance instead of triggering it.
  • Adaptive views that match the user’s cognitive state.
  • A model that supports ND users without imposing rigid structure.
  • A foundation for future mental‑health‑aligned productivity tools.
    • paper model: validated in real ND daily conditions stress‑tested under ND‑typical cognitive dynamics validated in environments with inconsistent routines and fluctuating regulation tested in conditions with multi‑day breaks and irregular engagement

SelfScape shows how neurocognitive modelling can transform planning for users who are underserved by traditional tools.

Although MAZE was designed for ND users, early applications in NT contexts (e.g., cognitive load management in language learning) show that its mechanisms generalise to NT users under stress, fatigue, or overload.
