SPINE Origin

How a decade of cognitive R&D crystallised into a formal framework

SPINE did not begin as a framework. It emerged from eight years of practice.

Between 2017 and 2024, I consistently worked in a pattern that combined systems thinking, cognitive modelling, emotional load analysis, and real‑world behavioural validation — long before I had language for it. I treated behaviour as a system shaped by constraints, regulation, and environment, and I designed interventions by modelling internal states rather than relying on surface‑level observations. At the time, this was simply “how my brain solved problems”.

The first large‑scale expression of this approach appeared in MAKO / Maths in New Clothes. Although SPINE did not yet exist as a formal structure, the project naturally followed its logic: grounding in system constraints, modelling cognitive profiles, mapping friction, designing interventions, and validating them in real classrooms. MAKO demonstrated that this implicit method could transform behaviour at scale — but it still lacked a formal backbone.

The turning point came in 2024–2025, during the high‑resolution behavioural forensics project in an enterprise environment. FEDRI successfully reconstructed a complex conflict, but the absence of a structured grounding layer revealed a critical gap: powerful cognitive tools require equally powerful ethical and contextual safeguards. Without a formalised process, high‑resolution analysis risked overwhelming participants, especially those at low regulation or with neurodivergent processing patterns.

This experience made the need for a unified architecture unmistakable.

SPINE was formally defined in 2025 as the backbone that integrates all layers of cognitive R&D:

  • the system context that shapes behaviour,
  • the predictive models that explain it,
  • the interventions that change it,
  • the neurocognitive patterns that constrain it,
  • and the real‑world environments that validate it.
What had been an intuitive method for years became a structured, ethical, and scalable framework — one capable of supporting CIR, FEDRI, and all subsequent cognitive engineering work.
